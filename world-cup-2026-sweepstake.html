How it works

Two teams. One combined score.

The 2026 World Cup runs 48 teams across 12 groups. The top two from each group plus the eight best third-placed sides reach a brand-new Round of 32, then it's straight knockout to the final on 19 July. Every entry is drawn one favourite (Pot A) and one outsider (Pot B). Your score is both teams added together as they progress — highest combined total at the final whistle wins.

Scoring

How points stack up

Each team scores for the furthest stage it reaches. Points are not cumulative — you only count the single highest stage a team gets to.

Worked example: a team that reaches the semi-final scores 20 points — not 5 + 10 + 15 + 20. Your entry total is your two teams added together, minus any red card deductions.

Red cards: −2 points for each red card shown to a player of either of your teams, at any stage, including a red from two yellows in the same match. An entry can finish on a negative score. Official FIFA records are the single source of truth.
